FAQs

Frequently Asked Questions I Get

  1. Do you speak any other languages?
    • At the moment, I speak a little bit of Malagasy and French. Reason why I speak a little is because across the places I lived, I surrounded myself with English speakers (I wish I had learned more French growing up instead of this).
  2. What is your favorite place you've been to?
    • I love the places I've been to, but probably Vancouver since that is my hometown. Leaving my home was a struggle, but I find it's easier to adapt to a new environment and country when you're younger and more mentally flexible. I plan on obtaining dual citizenship eventually.
  3. What are some pros and cons of traveling constantly? What is it like?
    • Pros:
      • You get to experience many cultures and become more educated about the real world. For example, experiencing different foods, languages, sports, clothing, etc.
      • I'm not nervous about moving homes or locations since I'm used to it my whole life.
      • A sense of independence; due to traveling constantly, patience and navigation skills are improved.
    • Cons:
      • You have to ditch your home and friends and make new friends constantly. It can set your mentality back and make you lag a bit in one's developmental years.
      • A sense of dependence; my father's job covered educational costs up until graduation from high school. The government would also assist you in finding you a house for wherever you're stationed/working, supplying you with health insurance, and even having a personal driver and maid in the house (when in third world countries).
